Sardine Cannery Artifacts

E – M – L Brand Sardine Can, packed by the Lawrence Canning Co., Rockland, Maine. ca. 1910. Collection of the Maine State Museum 2017.xx.

About this Artifact

The Sardine industry began in Maine in response to European demand for herring, a trade that was interrupted by the Franco-Prussian War of 1871-72. By 1900, sixty-nine Maine canneries operated in Eastport, Lubec, and other areas Down East. According to the Maine Department of Labor report in 1906, it was “very conservative” to count about 100 children under 14 years old working in the Eastport processing plants.
