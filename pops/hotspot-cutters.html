Cutters

Interior of a cutting shed with young cutters at work. Photograph by Lewis Hine, Eastport, Maine, August 1911. Courtesy of the Library of Congress.

This image shows 8-year old Clarence, and 9-year old Minnie at work. The children are paid by the “piece.” According to Lewis Hine, the children receive $.05 for filling the box on the low table with fish. In modern dollars that equals $1.25. Once they are finished, the children clean themselves and usually go to another job in the cannery.
