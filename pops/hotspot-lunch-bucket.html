Lunch Bucket

Lunch bucket, ca. 1900. Collection of the Maine State Museum, 80.11.1.

Workers, including children labored long hours requiring them to eat lunch and oftentimes dinner on the job. In 1847, the Maine Legislature passed the first laws restricting child labor. They legislated that children under 12 had to attend school for 4 months in the previous year to be employed in a mill, but many found ways around the law. The law did not limit the number of hours worked per day or week.
