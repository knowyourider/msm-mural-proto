Child Textile Workers

Continental Mills Card Room, photograph by Henri Larocque, Lewiston, Maine, 1885-1889. MHPC.S.3406.

These girls and women work tending the slubbing machines at the Continental Mill in Lewiston. After cotton fibers are straightened with carding machines, they are put in the cans that the girls stand behind. Slubbing machines then draw cotton strands out of the cans and twists it into desired thickness and yarn weight.
