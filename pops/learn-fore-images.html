Lewis Hine’s Photographs

Three young cutters at Seacoast Canning Co., Factory #4, Photograph by Lewis Hine, Eastport, Maine, August 1911. Courtesy of the Library of Congress.

About this Image

These boys, ages 10 to 12, work regularly at the canning factory. Lewis Hine quit his teaching job and went to work as a photographer for the National Child Labor Committee which documented children’s working conditions outside the home. Hine also focused on collecting the children’s stories. He spent August 1911 in sardine packing plants at Eastport and Lubec, Maine.
