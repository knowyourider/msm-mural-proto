Bandaged Hand

Richard Mills … showing a severely cut finger. Photograph by Lewis Hine, Eastport, Maine, August 1911. Courtesy of the Library of Congress.

Eight-year-old Richard cut his finger processing sardines in Eastport. He told the photographer that he was “very soon back to work, as it was the season.” He was the youngest of 11 children in the family, but only one of six still living. According to the 1910 U.S. Census, Richard’s 18 year-old brother worked in the cannery, but could not read or write. This raises the question if Richard or his 11 year-old brother were literate.
